Data curation

Curation principles

Data must be captured, standardized, organized, and made accessible to the scientific community in order to have a significant and lasting impact. The role of ViruSurf is to provide up-to-date, accurate, and accessible information, and, through this critical activity, facilitate virology scientific discovery.

Metadata curation

Our first curation contribution is to provide a unique schema (see Viral Conceptual Model) for different data sources. Different ad-hoc importer modules here (the code is available here) have been built to load GenBank, RefSeq, COG-UK, and GISAID data. Each source comes with different terminological choices when describing the different metadata. We have surveyed the the terms used in many sources to come up with appropriate reconciling solutions.



A complete mapping of our terms with the intergated sources can be found at... All results have been browsed and manually checked by the development team.

Specific curation efforts have been dedicated to:
  • Location information: when not available we add the GeoGroup (i.e., the continent or sub-continent)
  • Collection date: when the specific day is not available, we typically add 01 (the first day of the month) to make the item more easily searchable through date filters
  • Taxon name, ID, species: This is a critical field as it depends on the information retrieved through the authoritative NCBI Taxonomy. However, some viruses are described as "species" (such as SARS-CoV), some others as "sub-species" (such as SARS-CoV 2). This may result in confusion when comparing SARS-CoV vs. SARS-CoV 2. We have thus chosen a comprehensive approach. The field Taxon name refers to the most specific term used to define the type of virus under examination. The field Taxon ID is the related numerical identifier withing NCBI Taxon. Instead, Species indicates the ...
  • For ease of introperability with other resources, we also add the Host Taxon ID (which is added to the Host Taxon ID) manually.
  • From GenBank, not all the species of virus have a clearly specified reference sequence. In some cases we have defined the reference sequence by previously cross-checking with several research papers to ascertain that the typical reference sequence used for variant calling is defined.
  • The field Is Complete is computed for COG-UK following the same rules: if the sequence length is >= 95% of the reference length and if N% is lower than 5%, then Is Complete = True, ele Is Complete = false.
  • Strand, as well as Molecule Type, Is Single Stranded and Is Positive Stranded are curated manually.
  • GC% and N% have been computed to provide users with an additional information on the quality of the sequence.
  • For the Coverage field, a series of curation steps were necessary, as the values gathered from GenBank were very heterogeneous in the notation.
    • remove strings (possibly within parenthesis) that on the left/right of the numbers (including x, X, reads/nt, >, <.....)
    • commas or dots in between numbers should be treated in two different ways:
      • if they have three numbers on their right: remove
      • if they have less than three numbers on their right (so 1 or 2), treat those numbers as decimals and round the unit to the closest one (e.g. 112593.3 becomes 112593, whilst 70.8 becomes 71)
    These rules were applied after carefully checking many examples in the originary research papers to ascertain that our homogenization functions were consistent with the original semantics.
