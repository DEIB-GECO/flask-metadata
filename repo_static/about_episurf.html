How to cite

A paper that describes EpiSurf is currently under preparation. If you use EpiSurf or use screenshots generated with it please do ensure you credit our website (providing a link back to http://gmql.eu/episurf/)

If you use VirusViz, please cite our (recently accepted) paper:
A. Bernasconi, A. Gulino, T. Alfonsi, A. Canakoglu, P. Pinoli, A. Sandionigi, S. Ceri. VirusViz: Comparative analysis and effective visualization of viral nucleotide and amino acid variants. Accepted to Nucleic acids research - Web Server Issue 2021.

If you use data extracted from ViruSurf application, you may cite:
A. Canakoglu, P. Pinoli, A. Bernasconi, T. Alfonsi, D.P. Melidis, S. Ceri. 2021. ViruSurf: an integrated database to investigate viral sequences. Nucleic acids research, 49(D1), pp.D817-D824. https://doi.org/10.1093/nar/gkaa846

If you used the data model, you may cite:
A. Bernasconi, A. Canakoglu, P. Pinoli, S. Ceri. 2020, November. Empowering Virus Sequence Research Through Conceptual Modeling. In International Conference on Conceptual Modeling (pp. 388-402). Springer, Cham. https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-030-62522-1_29

Other data acknowledgements

EpiSurf includes sequences and metadata from GenBank in their Nuccore distribution from the Entrez APIs. The authors acknowledge the depositions of worldwide laboratories to GenBank and NMDC.

EpiSurf also includes sequences and metadata from COG-UK. The authors would like to acknowledge the COG-UK consortium for providing valuable data from across the UK, resulting from the work of hundreds of people. If you wish to publish research using the data, please get in contact with COG-UK first to ensure that those who have generated the data can be involved in its analysis. The COG-UK project is introduced in the Consortium paper.

Epitopes data is imported directly from the Immune Epitope DataBase (IEDB), which should be cited using the most recent publication. We deeply acknowledge its authors for their contribution to the scientific community.
